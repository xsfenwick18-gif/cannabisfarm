Start Here

The beginner's roadmap.

Eight stages, in order. Follow these and you'll understand every decision a grower makes — from day one to jar.

01

Choose Your Strain

Autoflower or photoperiod? Indica, sativa, or hybrid? Your choice here shapes everything else — growth time, light needs, and final effect.

02

Germinate Your Seed

Paper towel, glass of water, or straight to soil — getting the taproot out safely without stressing a seed that has never seen light.

03

Set Up Your Environment

Light, airflow, temperature, and humidity. Your grow space needs to hit specific targets before the plant goes in — not after problems start.

04

Seedling Stage

Gentle light, high humidity, minimal water. The seedling is fragile — your job is to do less, not more, and let it establish roots.

05

Vegetative Growth

Your plant builds its structure here. More light, more water, and optional training to shape the canopy for maximum bud sites later.

06

Flowering Stage

Buds form and fatten over 7–11 weeks. Environment stability matters most here — stress during flower directly costs you quality and yield.

07

Harvest at the Right Time

Read the trichomes under a loupe or microscope. Milky and amber trichomes on the buds — not the sugar leaves — tell you when to chop.

08

Dry, Cure & Enjoy

The final stage most beginners rush. A slow dry and a 4–8 week cure in sealed glass jars transforms good buds into exceptional ones.

Glossary

Know the terms.

Plain-English definitions for every term you'll encounter as a grower. Search by keyword.

AutoflowerA cannabis plant that flowers based on age rather than light cycle — typically beginning flower around day 30–40 regardless of how many hours of light it receives per day.
CanopyThe uppermost layer of leaves and bud sites on a plant. Managing canopy height and evenness is key to getting uniform light distribution across all your bud sites.
CuringThe post-drying process of storing trimmed buds in sealed glass jars to break down chlorophyll, stabilise terpenes, and improve flavour, aroma, and smoothness over 4–8 weeks.
DLIDaily Light Integral — the total amount of photosynthetically active light your plant receives over a full 24 hours. A more accurate measure of light than intensity alone.
FlushRunning plain, pH-adjusted water through your medium in the final weeks of flower to clear residual nutrients. Debated in organic growing where living soil self-regulates.
LSTLow Stress Training — bending and tying branches to create a flatter, wider canopy that exposes more bud sites to direct light without cutting or damaging the plant.
PhotoperiodA cannabis plant that switches from vegetative growth to flowering in response to a change in the daily light cycle — typically triggered by 12 hours of uninterrupted darkness.
PerliteA volcanic glass material added to soil to improve aeration and drainage. Creates air pockets that prevent compaction and give roots access to oxygen.
TerpenesAromatic compounds produced in the trichomes of cannabis that create its distinct flavours and aromas — and interact with cannabinoids to influence its effects.
TrichomesThe tiny crystal-like structures on buds and sugar leaves that produce and store cannabinoids (THC, CBD) and terpenes. Trichome colour indicates harvest readiness.
VPDVapour Pressure Deficit — a measurement combining temperature and humidity that indicates how hard your plant is working to push moisture through its leaves. Different growth stages need different VPD targets.
Worm CastingsThe excrement of earthworms — a slow-release organic fertiliser rich in microbes, enzymes, and plant-available nutrients. Adds nutrition and biological life to your soil.
