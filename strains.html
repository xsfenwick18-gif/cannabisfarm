How to evaluate a strain.

Six checks before you commit to a pack of seeds. Click any step for more detail.

01

Define Your Goal

Effect, yield, or grow time? Be honest about what you actually want before you fall for marketing names.

💡 Tip: write down your top priority in one word (relax, energise, yield, speed) before browsing seedbanks — it filters out 90% of the noise.

02

Check the Genetics

Is it a stable line, or an unstable new cross? Established lineage means more predictable results.

💡 Tip: search the strain name plus "phenotype" or "lineage" — reputable breeders publish this openly.

03

Read the Terpene Profile

Terpenes predict the actual smell and feel far better than the strain name or category ever will.

💡 Tip: myrcene-dominant = sedative/relaxing; limonene/pinene-dominant = uplifting/energising — a useful shortcut.

04

Check THC/CBD Ratio

Look for verified lab data, not just the breeder's marketing claim on the packet.

💡 Tip: be skeptical of any strain claiming 30%+ THC with no lab certificate attached.

05

Match to Your Setup

Autoflower or photoperiod, indoor height limits, outdoor climate tolerance — genetics must fit your space.

💡 Tip: check the breeder's stated flowering time and finished height against your actual tent/space dimensions.

06

Verify the Breeder

Reputable seedbanks with a track record beat an unknown source every time — germination rate depends on it.

💡 Tip: check grower forums for real germination-rate reports before buying from a new or unfamiliar breeder.

Glossary

Know the terms.

Plain-English definitions for strain and genetics terminology. Search by keyword.

CultivarA cultivated variety of cannabis bred and selected for specific traits — the more accurate term for what's casually called a "strain."
LandraceA cannabis population that developed naturally in a specific region over generations, without human hybridisation — the genetic ancestors of most modern strains.
PhenotypeThe observable traits (smell, structure, potency) an individual plant expresses — plants from the same seed pack can show different phenotypes.
GenotypeThe underlying genetic code of a plant, which determines the range of possible phenotypes it could express.
F1 HybridThe first-generation offspring of two distinct parent lines, often showing hybrid vigour — bred for consistency.
IBLInbred Line — a strain stabilised over many generations to breed true, producing consistent, predictable offspring.
Terpene ProfileThe specific combination and ratio of aromatic compounds in a strain, which shapes its smell, flavour, and contributes to its effect.
Entourage EffectThe theory that cannabinoids and terpenes work together synergistically, producing effects different from any single compound alone.
Hybrid VigourThe tendency for a cross between two distinct genetic lines to outperform either parent in traits like yield or resilience.
ChemovarA classification based on a plant's chemical profile (cannabinoids and terpenes) rather than its name or reported lineage — considered more scientifically accurate than "strain."
