Drying & Curing

This is the stage most beginners rush — and it's also the stage with the biggest impact on final smoothness, flavour, and potency.

Drying: the first step

After harvest, trimmed or whole branches should be hung in a dark room with stable conditions: roughly 18-22°C and 45-55% relative humidity, with gentle airflow (not direct fan exposure onto the buds, which dries them too unevenly and quickly). Avoid drying in spaces prone to humidity swings or mould risk, like garages or bathrooms.

A proper dry typically takes 7-14 days. You'll know it's ready to move to curing when small stems snap rather than bend — this indicates the right internal moisture level has been reached without over-drying.

Why darkness matters: light (especially UV) degrades cannabinoids and terpenes over time. Drying and curing should always happen away from direct light exposure to preserve potency and aroma.

Curing: where the real transformation happens

Once dried, buds are trimmed (if not already) and placed into airtight glass jars — 250-500ml jars are commonly used since they hold a manageable amount without too much empty headspace. Using a jar that's too large for the amount of bud inside increases air exposure and accelerates terpene loss.

During curing, residual moisture inside the bud equalises throughout, chlorophyll continues breaking down (reducing the "grassy" taste raw cannabis can have), and terpenes stabilise — all of which noticeably improve smoothness, flavour, and aroma compared to bud that's only been dried.

The burping schedule

"Burping" means briefly opening the jars to exchange air and release excess humidity. A standard schedule is:

  • Week 1: Burp twice daily, for a few minutes each time, checking for any unwanted moisture or smell.
  • After week 1: Reduce to once daily, then eventually a few times a week as the cure stabilises.

A minimum cure of 6 weeks is widely recommended for noticeably better results than uncured bud, though many growers continue curing for months, with quality continuing to improve gradually over that time.

Signs something's wrong

If you notice an ammonia-like smell, it usually indicates the bud was jarred while still too moist. If you see any mould (often white or grey fuzzy patches, distinct from trichomes), remove the affected bud immediately and increase burping frequency or airflow for the rest of the batch to prevent it from spreading.
