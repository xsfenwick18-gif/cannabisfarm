Getting Started & Germination

Every grow starts the same way: a seed, a decision, and a bit of patience. Here's what actually matters in week one.

Choosing your first strain

For a first grow, simplicity beats hype. Autoflowering strains are generally the most forgiving choice for beginners because they don't depend on a strict light schedule to start flowering, and they finish faster — usually within 10-12 weeks from seed to harvest. Photoperiod strains give you more control over size and training but demand more attention to light timing.

Look for strains described as "beginner friendly," "resilient," or "high vigor" in seed descriptions. Avoid delicate, high-THC boutique genetics for your first run — they tend to be less forgiving of small mistakes.

Germination methods

There are three common ways to germinate a cannabis seed, and all of them work when done correctly:

  • Paper towel method: Sandwich the seed between two damp (not soaked) paper towels, keep it warm and dark, and check daily. A taproot usually appears within 2-7 days.
  • Glass of water: Soak the seed in room-temperature water for 12-24 hours until it sinks, then move it to a damp paper towel or directly to soil.
  • Direct to soil: Plant the seed about 1-1.5cm deep in pre-moistened soil. This is the lowest-stress method since the seedling never has to be transplanted.
Important for autoflowers: if you're growing autos, plant directly into your final pot. Autoflowers are sensitive to root disturbance, and transplanting can set back their growth permanently since they don't have time to recover on their fixed timeline.

The taproot rule

Once you see a taproot (a small white root emerging from the seed), don't wait for it to grow long. Plant it as soon as it reaches about 0.5-1cm. A longer taproot is more fragile and easily damaged during planting, and damaged roots are one of the most common causes of failed germination.

First week care

Newly emerged seedlings need warmth (around 22-26°C), high humidity (you can use a humidity dome or a cut plastic bottle over the pot for the first 5-7 days), and gentle, low-intensity light. Avoid feeding any nutrients in the first 1-2 weeks — the seed itself contains enough energy to get the seedling started, and overfeeding this early is a common beginner mistake that causes more harm than underfeeding.
