Flowering & Harvest

Harvest timing is one of the few decisions you can't undo. Getting it right makes the difference between good and exceptional bud.

The flowering timeline

Once flowering begins (either by light schedule change for photoperiods, or automatically around day 30-40 for autoflowers), most strains take roughly 7-11 weeks to fully mature, though this varies significantly by strain — sativa-dominant strains tend toward the longer end, indica-dominant strains toward the shorter end. Seed bank descriptions usually list an estimated flowering time as a starting reference point.

Reading trichomes

The single most reliable way to judge harvest readiness is examining trichomes — the small, crystal-like structures on the buds themselves — using a loupe or small microscope (cheap 60x-100x loupes work fine). Trichomes progress through three visual stages:

  • Clear: Trichomes are translucent and glassy. The plant isn't ready yet — cannabinoid production is still increasing.
  • Milky/cloudy: Trichomes turn opaque and white. This generally indicates peak THC content.
  • Amber: Trichomes take on an amber or golden tint as THC slowly degrades into other compounds, generally associated with a more sedating effect.

A widely used target is roughly 70% milky and 30% amber trichomes, which balances potency with the mellower effect amber trichomes are associated with — though personal preference varies, and some growers harvest earlier (more clear/milky) for a more energetic effect, or later (more amber) for a heavier one.

Check the right spot: always examine trichomes on the buds themselves, not the surrounding sugar leaves — sugar leaf trichomes mature differently and can be misleading if used alone to judge timing.

To flush or not to flush

Flushing means watering with plain, pH-balanced water (no nutrients) for the final 1-2 weeks before harvest, intended to clear any residual nutrient salts from the growing medium. This is more commonly practiced with synthetic nutrient feeding. In organic, living-soil setups, many growers skip flushing entirely, since the soil's biology naturally regulates nutrient availability and there's less concern about residual salts to begin with.

The actual harvest

Once trichomes indicate readiness, harvest by cutting branches or the whole plant at the base, then move directly into the drying process — ideally without significant delay, since cut cannabis begins to degrade in quality if left sitting in poor conditions.
