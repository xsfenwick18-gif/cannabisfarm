01

Ancient Use

Cannabis cultivation traces back thousands of years in Central Asia, used for fibre, food, medicine, and ritual long before it became controversial.

💡 Archaeological evidence of cannabis use has been found in burial and ritual sites across ancient China and Central Asia, some dating back over 2,500 years.

02

Trade Routes & Spread

Cannabis moved along ancient trade routes into the Middle East, Africa, and India, adapting into countless local medicinal and spiritual traditions.

💡 In India, cannabis (bhang) became embedded in religious festivals and Ayurvedic medicine — a tradition still practiced today.

03

Early Medical Use

By the 1800s, cannabis extracts were common in Western pharmacies, prescribed for pain, convulsions, and a wide range of ailments.

💡 Cannabis tinctures were listed in the United States Pharmacopeia for decades before prohibition began.

04

Criminalisation

The early-to-mid 20th century saw cannabis criminalised across much of the world, often driven more by politics than by medical evidence.

💡 The 1961 UN Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs pushed prohibition into a global standard, binding most member states.

05

The War on Drugs

From the 1970s onward, aggressive enforcement policy led to mass incarceration and deep social stigma that persists in many countries today.

💡 This era is widely credited with entrenching much of the misinformation about cannabis that took decades to unwind.

06

Legalisation & South Africa Today

From the 1990s medical wave to today's recreational reform — including South Africa's 2018 Constitutional Court ruling protecting private use, and the 2024 Cannabis for Private Purposes Act.

💡 South Africa's shift began with the 2018 Prince judgment, which found the criminalisation of private cannabis use and cultivation unconstitutional.

Glossary

Know the terms.

Plain-English definitions for history and legal terminology. Search by keyword.

ProhibitionThe period during which cannabis was made illegal to grow, possess, or use, enforced through criminal law rather than regulation.
DecriminalisationRemoving criminal penalties for personal possession/use, without necessarily making sale or commercial supply legal.
LegalisationFull removal of legal prohibition, typically including a regulated framework for cultivation, sale, and taxation.
Prince Judgment (2018)The South African Constitutional Court ruling that decriminalised the private use, possession, and cultivation of cannabis by adults.
Cannabis for Private Purposes ActSouth African legislation (2024) that formalised the legal framework for private cannabis use and home cultivation following the Prince judgment.
Single Convention on Narcotic DrugsA 1961 United Nations treaty that classified cannabis as a controlled substance, shaping prohibition policy across most of the world for decades.
HempCannabis grown specifically for fibre, seed, or low-THC industrial use — legally distinct from "marijuana" in many jurisdictions based on THC content.
Rastafari & GanjaCannabis ("ganja") holds sacramental significance in the Rastafari faith, used in meditation and religious ritual since the movement's origins in Jamaica.
Schedule I / Schedule 7Drug classification categories used in various countries (e.g. US, SA) historically placing cannabis among the most restricted substances, often disputed as outdated.
Marijuana Tax Act (1937)Early US federal legislation that effectively criminalised cannabis through prohibitive taxation, widely seen as the start of formal US prohibition.
